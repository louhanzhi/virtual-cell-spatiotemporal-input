## In One Sentence
A high-level Review of how recent MS-based proteomics technology (sample prep, instrumentation, DIA/targeted acquisition, AI) has expanded the field from bulk analysis to single-cell proteomics, spatial profiling, interaction/PTM/structural/chemo-proteomics, and clinical body-fluid and tissue diagnostics, with an outlook on AI and proteomics "foundation models."

## Orientation (non-binding)
This is the home-turf, capability-side paper for our perspective: it is authored by the Guo/Steen/Mann groups and documents that high-throughput single-cell and spatial proteomics are now feasible (most relevant to roadmap claim C6, Axis A-proteome, Axis C-spatial). It is also a rich source of orthogonality ammunition — transcriptome–proteome divergence, proteins' higher copy number/dynamic range, PTM/interaction/metabolite layers RNA cannot report (C4, C5, Axis A). Its clinical sections supply standards/comparability material (C9: workflow intensities not comparable, underpowered cohorts, raw-data deposition). Its outlook explicitly frames perturbation proteomics as a candidate substrate for cell foundation models (C1, C4). It can serve as BOTH support (proteomics fills the protein/space gaps) and as a self-aware foil (it concedes single-cell proteomics is "currently limited to expression proteomics," that measurement cannot amplify, and that data are not cross-comparable).

> "As the direct effector of biological functions and phenotypes, the proteome reflects the complex interplay between genetic predisposition, environmental factors and lifestyle influences. Note that unlike transcriptomics, proteomics cannot amplify the quantity measured; however, there is typically more than 100-fold more protein than transcript for every expressed gene, and those proteins reflect the cell-to-cell variance much better."
